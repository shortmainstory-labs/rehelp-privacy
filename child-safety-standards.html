Child Safety Standards

ReHelp - Short Main Story Labs

Last Updated: January 30, 2026

Zero Tolerance Policy

ReHelp and Short Main Story Labs have ZERO TOLERANCE for Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE). We are committed to protecting children and maintaining the highest standards of child safety across all our platforms and services.

1. Our Commitment to Child Safety

ReHelp is a personal safety and emergency alert application designed to connect users with their trusted circle of family and friends. While our app is designed for users aged 13 and above, we recognize our responsibility to protect all users, especially minors, from any form of abuse or exploitation.

We strictly prohibit and will actively prevent:

  • Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM) in any form
  • Child grooming or predatory behavior
  • Sexual exploitation of children
  • Trafficking or solicitation involving minors
  • Any content, communication, or behavior that endangers children

2. Prohibited Content and Behavior

2.1 Strictly Forbidden Activities

The following activities are ABSOLUTELY PROHIBITED on ReHelp:

  • Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM): Any imagery, video, or content depicting or suggesting sexual abuse of children
  • Grooming: Any attempt to build trust with a minor for the purpose of sexual abuse or exploitation
  • Solicitation: Requesting sexual content, images, or activities from minors
  • Sextortion: Threatening to share intimate images of minors or coercing minors into producing such content
  • Trafficking: Recruiting, harboring, or transporting minors for exploitation
  • Inappropriate Communication: Sexually explicit messages or communication with minors
  • Sexualization of Minors: Content that sexualizes children in any way

2.2 User-Generated Content Restrictions

While ReHelp is primarily designed for emergency alerts and does not facilitate user-generated public content, we maintain strict policies:

  • Medical card information must not contain inappropriate content
  • Display names and profile customization must not reference, suggest, or promote CSAE
  • Alert messages must not be misused for inappropriate communication with minors

3. Age Verification and Restrictions

3.1 Minimum Age Requirement

  • Minimum Age: ReHelp is designed for users aged 13 years and older
  • Parental Consent: Users aged 13-17 should use ReHelp with parental knowledge and consent
  • Under 13: ReHelp is NOT intended for children under 13 years of age

3.2 Age-Appropriate Design

ReHelp is designed with age-appropriate safeguards:

  • No public forums or chat features that could enable predatory behavior
  • Trusted circle connections require mutual consent and existing relationships
  • Emergency-focused functionality minimizes social networking risks
  • Privacy-first design with no public profiles or searchable directories

4. Detection and Prevention Measures

4.1 Proactive Monitoring

We implement multiple layers of protection:

  • Account Monitoring: Automated systems to detect suspicious patterns or behavior
  • Content Filtering: Prevention of inappropriate content in user-customizable fields
  • Abuse Detection: Monitoring for misuse of the emergency alert system
  • Regular Audits: Periodic review of platform usage to identify and address risks

4.2 Technical Safeguards

  • No anonymous accounts - ReHelp IDs provide accountability
  • Closed network design - users can only connect with people they trust
  • No public messaging or forums
  • Minimal data retention to reduce exploitation risks
  • Encrypted communications (future implementation)

5. Reporting Mechanisms

5.1 How to Report CSAE Concerns

If you suspect or witness child sexual abuse or exploitation on ReHelp:

Email: childsafety@rehelp.app

Response Time: We investigate all reports within 24 hours and take immediate action when CSAE is suspected.

5.2 What to Include in Your Report

  • ReHelp ID of the account(s) involved
  • Description of the concerning behavior or content
  • Date and time of incident (if known)
  • Any screenshots or evidence (if safe to provide)
  • Your contact information (kept confidential)

Anonymous Reporting: You can report anonymously if you prefer. We will still investigate all reports thoroughly.

5.3 Emergency Situations

If a child is in immediate danger:

  • Call local emergency services immediately:
    • United States: 911
    • European Union: 112
    • United Kingdom: 999
  • Report to NCMEC (USA): CyberTipline
  • Report to INTERPOL: Via your local law enforcement

6. Our Response to Reports

6.1 Investigation Process

When we receive a report of potential CSAE:

  • Immediate Review: All reports are prioritized and reviewed within 24 hours
  • Account Suspension: Suspected accounts are immediately suspended pending investigation
  • Evidence Preservation: Relevant data is preserved for law enforcement
  • Expert Assessment: Reports are reviewed by trained child safety specialists

6.2 Actions We Take

Upon confirmation of CSAE violations:

  • Permanent Ban: Immediate and permanent removal from ReHelp
  • Law Enforcement Reporting: Mandatory reporting to NCMEC (National Center for Missing & Exploited Children) and appropriate authorities
  • Evidence Provision: Full cooperation with law enforcement investigations
  • Network Protection: Prevention of re-registration by banned users

7. Cooperation with Authorities

7.1 Mandatory Reporting

We are committed to reporting all suspected CSAE to appropriate authorities:

  • United States: Reports filed with NCMEC's CyberTipline as required by federal law
  • International: Cooperation with INTERPOL and local law enforcement agencies
  • Response Time: Reports submitted within 24 hours of discovery

7.2 Law Enforcement Cooperation

  • Immediate response to law enforcement requests
  • Preservation of evidence in active investigations
  • Technical assistance to investigators
  • Ongoing communication throughout investigations

8. User Education and Awareness

8.1 Educating Users

We are committed to educating our community:

  • Safety Resources: In-app guides on recognizing and reporting abuse
  • Parental Guidance: Resources for parents to protect their children
  • Teen Safety: Age-appropriate safety education for adolescent users
  • Regular Updates: Ongoing communication about safety features and policies

8.2 Warning Signs of Grooming

We educate users to recognize potential grooming behaviors:

  • Adults seeking private communication with minors
  • Requests for personal information or photos
  • Attempts to isolate minors from family/friends
  • Offering gifts, money, or special attention
  • Introducing sexual topics into conversations
  • Requests to keep relationships secret

9. Parental Controls and Family Safety

9.1 For Parents and Guardians

ReHelp supports parental involvement:

  • Trusted Circle Oversight: Parents can be part of their child's trusted circle
  • Emergency Alerts: Parents receive all emergency alerts from their children
  • Location Sharing: Parents can see their child's location during emergencies
  • Account Review: Parents should periodically review their child's connections

9.2 Recommended Practices for Families

  • Discuss ReHelp's purpose and proper use with children
  • Ensure children only connect with known, trusted individuals
  • Monitor who is in your child's trusted circle
  • Have ongoing conversations about online safety
  • Establish rules for emergency app usage

10. Platform Design for Child Safety

10.1 Safety by Design

ReHelp is architected with child safety as a core principle:

  • No Public Profiles: Users cannot be searched or discovered by strangers
  • Closed Network: Connections require mutual consent
  • Emergency Focus: Platform designed for safety, not social networking
  • Minimal Data Collection: Reduces risk of data exploitation
  • Privacy-First: No tracking or data selling that could expose minors
  • No Messaging: No open messaging system that could be abused

10.2 Future Safety Enhancements

We are continuously improving child safety:

  • Implementation of end-to-end encryption
  • Advanced behavioral analysis to detect abuse patterns
  • Age verification improvements
  • Enhanced parental control features
  • Partnership with child safety organizations

11. Staff Training and Accountability

11.1 Team Training

All ReHelp team members receive:

  • Comprehensive child safety training
  • CSAE recognition and reporting procedures
  • Regular updates on emerging threats
  • Trauma-informed response training

11.2 Internal Accountability

  • Designated Child Safety Officer
  • Regular policy reviews and updates
  • Incident response drills and preparedness
  • Third-party audits of safety measures

12. Transparency and Reporting

12.1 Transparency Commitments

We are committed to transparency regarding child safety:

  • Annual Reports: Public reporting of CSAE prevention efforts
  • Statistics: Aggregate data on reports received and actions taken (no identifying information)
  • Policy Updates: Public notification of any changes to safety standards
